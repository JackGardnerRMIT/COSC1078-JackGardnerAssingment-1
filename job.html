A description of a Penetration Tester

The position of a Junior Penetration Tester for Shea involves testing web application and software’s security functions, you aim to find any flaws in the security and assist in patching the issues that allowed for exploitation of the software. The job will include a lot of testing of the software, it will have a process of trial and error, attempting multiple methods to exploit software. The reason I find this job appealing is because I think it would be interesting and challenging to attempt to penetrate software. It would also be rewarding if you did find exploits and were successful in your job. Another reason is high pay, experienced penetration testers often are paid over 100 thousand AUD per year. It would also require little travel and would allow the worker to do their job in the comforts of their own home.

Skills, qualifacations and experince required to become a Penetration Tester

There are many skills, qualifications and experience required to become a Junior Penetration Tester as Shea. Some of the skills required to work this job include clear communication and dedication. Clear communication is a key skill that is essential for when working with teams of other employees at Shea, it is also important when making written and verbal reports of Penetration Testing results and speaking to managers for tasks. Dedication is also a highly important skill which would be necessary for the longevity and success of your career. Dedicated study and a high work ethic specific to the field of Cyber-Security can led to being a better Penetration Tester, it also shows that you are willing to spend lots of time working at your job and that you are passionate about it. There is no official qualification required, such as a University Degree; however, some abilities what are requited include being “Proficient in Linux, Mac or Windows command lines”, “A general understanding of the HTTP protocol” and “Proven coding or scripting skills in one or more languages, for example: TypeScript, Java, C#, Ruby, Python or Shell scripting such as Bash.” All these abilities include being able to understand computing protocol or speaking a programming language, which is required to be able to do Penetration Testing. Some experience required is that you must “have previously built a web application either professionally or as a hobbyist.” This experience will help the employee understand how to do their job and how web applications function.
